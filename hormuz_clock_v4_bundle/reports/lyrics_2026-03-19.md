# Lyrics — Hormuz Clock, 2026-03-19

## Verse 1
Four ships through the narrows where a hundred thirty-eight used to glide,
Static in the sextant, hot metal on the tide.
Engines hum a prayer while the signal ghosts collide,
And the brokers light cigars with the premiums they hide.

## Chorus
Good morning, blue horizon, red warning in the spray,
The clock says hold the line, the line says drift away.
Stocks can buy a little dawn, but they cannot part the gray,
So sing it to the tankers: not open, not today.

## Verse 2
Coverage still breathing, but it costs a captain's sleep,
One percent, one and a half, for a promise they might keep.
Pipelines make a side road, but the cut is far from deep,
And every blinking waypoint has a haunted little beep.

## Bridge
Escort plans are whispering that maybe dawn can form,
But missiles hit the shoreline and the market smells the storm.
So the dial keeps two truths alive at once in uniform:
A lane that wants reopening, a region bending warm.

## Outro
Good morning to the lookouts.
Good morning to the screens.
Good morning to the narrow strait between the price and means.
The song is not a forecast.
The song is just the seam:
Where facts hit the waterline,
and priors start to gleam.
